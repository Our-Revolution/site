The Problem

Drug companies are overcharging taxpayers by billions of dollars each year for life-saving drugs. Profits over human life has become a terrible trend within the pharmaceutical industry, and your help in supporting this act will be the igniter that helps put an end to it.

The Solution

The California Drug Price Relief Act (Prop 61) is a 2016 ballot initiative that will require the State of California to pay no more for prescription drugs than is paid for the same medication by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (DVA), which provides healthcare services for nearly 22 million U.S. military veterans.

By obtaining equivalent savings as the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, California Taxpayers will have affordable access to prescription drugs; drugs that are life saving. The Drug Price Relief Act would save California taxpayers approximately $5.7 billion over 10 years.
